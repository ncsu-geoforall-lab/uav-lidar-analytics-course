Topic 5: Advanced analytics

A. UAS and lidar data: comparison, fusion and analysis

Outline:

  • Analysis of lidar and UAS DSMs differences
  • Patching and smooth fusion of UAS and lidar DSM
  • Overland flow simulation on fused DEM

Lecture:

  • Lecture slides
  • Lecture recordings: Theory and Workflow, Smooth DEM/DSM fusion, and Overland flow modeling
Supplemental materials:
  • Drone LiDAR vs Photogrammetry: A Technical Guide, brief article from SPAR3D (online news on 3D technology: sensing, processing and visualization)
  • Fusion of multi-scale DEMs using a regularized super-resolution method. Linwei Yue , Huanfeng Shen , Qiangqiang Yuan , Liangpei Zhang. International Journal of Geographical Information Science. Vol. 29, Iss. 12, 2015
  • Petrasova et al. 2017, Fusion of high-resolution DEMs for water flow modeling. Open Geospatial Data, Software and Standards 2:6.

Assignment 5A

  • Fusion of UAS and lidar data
  • Optional, advanced topic: Python Scripting for UAS/lidar analysis in GRASS GIS

Homework:

Prepare report on differences between lidar and UAS elevation data, their fusion and impact of different fusion/patching techniques on overland flow modeling.

B. Analysis of multitemporal UAS data and its applications

mapping plant growth, soil erosion and other phenomena, temporal data framework, python

Outline:

  • monitoring landscape changes using UAS
  • introduction to temporal data framework
  • basic analysis of 3D time series
  • applications: monitoring plant growth, coastal erosion, soil erosion, flooding

Lecture:

  • Lecture slides
  • Lecture recordings: Introduction and Analysis
Supplemental materials:
  • Spatio-temporal data handling and visualization in GRASS GIS (FOSS4G 2014 workshop)
  • GIS-based Analysis of Coastal Lidar Time-Series, Springer brief available for free through NCSU libraries

Assignment 5B

  • analyze multitemporal UAS data

Homework:

Prepare report on mapping plant growth, viewsheds and water flow patterns using ultrahigh resolution DEMs.
